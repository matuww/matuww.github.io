Glagolitic

Ⰳⰾⰰⰳⱁⰾⰻⱌⰰ

Ⰳⰾⰰⰳⱁⰾⰻⱌⰰ

Alphabet1
862/3 CE - 19 c. CE1-3


Left-to-right1

Languages1,2

Old Church Slavonic (including regional recensions), Church Slavonic

Places2

Moravia, Bulgaria, Macedonia, Croatia (especially in Istria and Dalmatia), Bohemia, and Poland.

Abecedary1,3-6

The brothers St. Cyril and St. Methodius were Byzantine Christian theologians who created the Glagolitic alphabet.
Letter order is based on Greek.
Minuscules take the same shape of majuscules and are of a smaller size.

Rounded form

Also known as Obla (Ⱁⰱⰾⰰ) or Bulgarian Glagolitic.
