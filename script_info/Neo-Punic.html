Epigraphy2

Limestone frieze with inscription in Neo-Punic (6 ll); half of a bilingual inscription, the other half being Numidian (Libyco-Berber) (1852,0305.2); instrumental in deciphering Numidian script which is alphabetic and reads from right to left like Punic; from punic style mausoleum dedicated to Ateban, a Numidian prince; cracked; small areas of surface loss; brown staining near top.
